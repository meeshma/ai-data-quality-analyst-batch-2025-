Barbell Squats

Muscles Worked

  • Quadriceps (Quads)
  • Gluteus Maximus (Glutes)
  • Hamstrings
  • Core (Abdominals & Obliques)
  • Lower Back (Erector Spinae)

How to Perform

  • Set the bar on a squat rack at mid-chest height and step under it so it sits across your upper traps (or slightly lower on the rear delts for a low-bar variation).
  • Grip the bar just outside shoulder width, squeeze your shoulder blades together, and lift the bar off the rack by standing up.
  • Take a small step back, set your stance (approximately shoulder-width), and brace your core.
  • Initiate the squat by pushing your hips back and bending your knees, keeping chest up and spine neutral.
  • Descend until your thighs are at least parallel to the floor (or deeper if your mobility allows).
  • Drive through your heels, extend hips and knees, and return to standing while maintaining a tight core.
  • Carefully rack the bar when your set is complete.

Tips

  • Keep your knees tracking over your toes (don't let them cave inward).
  • Maintain a neutral spine and a proud chest — don't round your lower back.
  • Breathe in and brace your core before the descent; exhale as you ascend.
  • Use an appropriate stance for your hip anatomy — experiment slightly wider or narrower.
  • Focus on sitting back into your hips rather than only bending the knees.

Common Mistakes

  • Rounding the lower back (butt wink) at the bottom of the squat.
  • Pulling the bar down onto the neck — position it across traps/upper-back, not the neck.
  • Letting knees collapse inward (valgus collapse).
  • Pushing weight onto toes instead of driving through heels.
  • Using too much weight before mastering form.
