Hanging Leg Raise

Muscles Worked

  • Lower Abs (Primary)
  • Hip Flexors
  • Obliques (Stabilization)
  • Forearms & Grip Strength
  • Lats (Stabilization)

How to Perform

  • Hang from a pull-up bar with arms fully extended and legs straight.
  • Brace your core and keep your body still—avoid swinging.
  • Lift your legs upward until they form a 90° angle with your torso.
  • Squeeze your abs at the top of the movement.
  • Lower your legs slowly and with full control.

Tips

  • Keep your pelvis tucked to engage lower abs more.
  • Avoid swinging; keep the movement strict.
  • Start with knee raises if straight-leg raises are too difficult.
  • Grip the bar tightly to maintain stability.

Common Mistakes

  • Using momentum to lift legs.
  • Swinging the body excessively.
  • Not lowering the legs fully on each rep.
  • Bending elbows or shrugging shoulders.
